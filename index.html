Overview

I fit everything into a backpack and a large duffel bag with wheels. They shuffle you pretty fast, so bring a duffel that you can wear as a backpack. Avoid loud rolling suitcases. Label everything with your last name. Keep things basic and conservative, and aim to be as low-maintenance as possible.

This document can feel overwhelming—breathe, and ask questions if you have any!

Personal Items

  • Laptop, cell phone, chargers, headphones, portable charger
  • Wallet with debit card (at least $500) and ID
  • Orders and personal documents (COVID card!) – included in the envelope from your OSO

Clothing

  • 2 polo shirts (long sleeve for winter — long hours outdoors during processing)
  • 3–4 pairs white undershirts
  • 2 pairs khaki pants & a belt
  • White socks (you’ll also buy more there a few weeks in)
  • Undergarments: underwear, sports bras (about a week’s worth)
  • A sweatshirt (optional) & jacket
  • Green PT gear (bring if you have it; you’ll buy shirts/shorts there regardless—consider extras)
  • At least 6 pairs Fox River socks in Coyote Brown

Shoes

  • 1 pair comfortable dress shoes
  • 2 pairs broken-in running shoes
  • 1–2 pairs broken-in boots (Bates Lites or Danner Reckonings, USMC EGA)
  • Black shower shoes

Hygiene

  • 1 white towel; hygiene kit/bag for carrying items to the head
  • Toothbrush & toothpaste (optional: Colgate Wisps or mouthwash for the field)
  • Floss or floss picks; deodorant
  • 3-in-1 shampoo/conditioner/bodywash; soap; lotion; chapstick
  • Q-tips (long for rifle cleaning and short)
  • Small sunscreen; small foot powder; baby wipes

Writing & Supplies

  • Kendall Kovidian 1-inch waterproof tape (3+ rolls; more if long last name)
  • Clear packing tape (for laminating); black electrical tape
  • Storage clipboard; plastic dividers (line up nametapes on dividers inside clipboard)
  • Quart and gallon ziplock bags
  • Spiral flash cards (if that’s your style); notebook; pencil pouch
  • Black Frixion erasable pens (bring a box—ink runs out fast)
  • Mechanical pencils (work in Rite-in-the-Rain); green highlighter (visible under red moonbeam)
  • Sharpies (you’ll get more at OCS); 6-inch ruler; scissors
  • 3 Rite-in-the-Rain notebooks + cover (cheaper if bought beforehand)

Gear

  • Conservative waterproof watch (e.g., inexpensive Casio)
  • 2 moonbeams with red light (headlamps)
  • Extra AAA batteries
  • Boot bands

Optional: Self-Care

  • Blister kit; bandaids; cooling squares; Neosporin
  • Lotrimin ultra; foot powder; cough drops; Body Glide
  • Eye drops; foot spray; lacrosse ball (for feet)

More Optional

  • Field mirror; M16 bore snake
  • Hand warmers (winter; bulk at BJs/Costco)
  • Insoles for running shoes/boots (preference)
  • Small bottle of wrinkle release
  • Bible, notes, pictures (keep sentimentals in Rite-in-the-Rain pocket)
  • Small pouch for pack to keep field hygiene items

Female Candidates

  • Hair items: brushes, hair ties, elastics, bobby pins, plenty of gel (busted hair = chits & essays)
  • Leave-in conditioner if wearing a bun; learn to tie a perfect bun quickly before OCS
  • 1 box tampons/pads (cycles may vary under training stress)
  • Sports bras

Making Name Tapes

You must label everything with clean 6" name tapes. Always have fresh ones prepared. Label format: last name, company (Alpha, Bravo, etc.), and platoon number.

  • Prepare 6" tapes with your last name centered and leave space for platoon (assigned at check-in), or pre-cut a stack of 6" strips.
  • Use Kendall Kovidian 1" waterproof tape, stencil kit and sharpies (stencil & sharpies are bought at OCS).
  • Store nametapes on plastic dividers inside a binder or storage clipboard for quick access.

Preparation

  • Make nametapes
  • Get familiar with academics (OCS Blog outline)
  • Know the OPORD (OSMEAC) and draft a sample skeleton
  • Study fire team tactics and formations
  • Learn leadership principles and traits
  • Watch M16A4 service rifle and drill videos
  • Practice military-style bed & rolling sleeves
  • Run in boots beforehand (lightly—avoid shin splints; ~½ mile)
  • Study rank structure/insignia

Rank Insignia & Greetings

You should be able to identify rank immediately to greet Marines properly and respectfully.

Examples

  • “Good ______, Gunnery Sergeant/Ladies/Gentlemen. Candidate _____ requests to speak to Gunnery Sergeant ______.”
  • “Good ______, Gunnery Sergeant/Ladies/Gentlemen. Candidate _____ reporting to Gunnery Sergeant ______ as ordered.”

Tip: Time-Based Greetings

  • Before morning chow: “Good morning, GySgt.”
  • After afternoon chow: “Good afternoon, GySgt.”
  • After evening chow: “Good evening, GySgt.”

Miscellaneous Advice

  • Share your mailing address with family ASAP before phones are taken.
  • Sleep on top of your sheets to avoid remaking the rack; use the issued woobie (poncho liner).
  • Phones & liberty return on Week 3—lean on your support system.
  • If you consider dropping, push to the end of the week—liberty often resets your perspective.
  • Always have 3 PT rolls ready (towel with skivvy shorts, shirt, and undergarments rolled inside).
  • Nightly: stretch, roll out; ankle alphabet in rack; ice as needed.
  • Foot care: change socks often and use foot powder.
  • Practice O-Course techniques (chicken wing / college boy roll).
  • Maintain bearing: never smile or look around unnecessarily.
  • Memorize your rifle serial #; always carry note-taking gear.
  • When in a billet, care for your platoon—not just your own pass.
  • Eat fast, hydrate constantly (1.5–2 camelbaks/day).

Staff

  • Address Sergeant Instructors confidently (be loud, clear; avoid “um/uh”).
  • SI’s job is to convince you to go home; your job is to prove you deserve to stay.
  • Assume staff know more and see everything. Own mistakes, improve, and maintain integrity.
